Jehovah — The True God

Jehovah is the personal name of the true God, the Creator of all things.
His name appears nearly 7,000 times in the original Bible text.
“I am Jehovah. That is my name.” — Isaiah 42:8
“May people know that you, whose name is Jehovah, you alone are the Most High over all the earth.” — Psalm 83:18
Jesus made Jehovah's name known to his followers.
“I have made your name manifest to the men whom you gave me out of the world.” — John 17:6
“I have made your name known to them, and will make it known.” — John 17:26
Jehovah is the Almighty, eternal, and loving God who invites us to use His name and draw close to Him.

Bible Translation — The New World Translation

The New World Translation is an accurate, modern-language translation of the Holy Scriptures.
It was revised in 2013 for greater clarity and to touch the heart of readers.
It uses the oldest texts available to stay close to the originals.
For the Hebrew Scriptures, it draws from ancient sources like the Dead Sea Scrolls (dating from the 3rd century B.C.E. to the 1st century C.E., over 1,000 years older than later copies) and the Septuagint (Greek translation starting in the 3rd–2nd century B.C.E.).
For the Greek Scriptures, it relies on early manuscripts like Codex Sinaiticus and Codex Vaticanus (mid-4th century C.E., among the oldest complete copies, close to the time of the apostles, within about 300 years of Jesus' earthly ministry).
“All Scripture is inspired of God and beneficial for teaching...” — 2 Timothy 3:16

Jesus Christ — God's Son and King

Jesus is the only-begotten Son of God, sent to fulfill Jehovah's purpose.
Jesus Christ is God's Son; he is not God himself.
He is the designated King of God's Kingdom.
“This means everlasting life, their coming to know you, the only true God, and the one whom you sent, Jesus Christ.” — John 17:3
“He will rule as king... and there will be no end to his Kingdom.” — Luke 1:33
Jesus perfectly reflected Jehovah and taught about God's Kingdom more than any other subject.

The Ransom — Jesus' Sacrifice

Jehovah sent his son Jesus to give his perfect human life as a ransom to free mankind from sin and death inherited from Adam.
This ransom provides the basis for forgiveness and everlasting life.
“The Son of man came... to give his life as a ransom in exchange for many.” — Matthew 20:28
“There is one God, and one mediator between God and men, a man, Christ Jesus, who gave himself a corresponding ransom for all.” — 1 Timothy 2:5-6
Jesus commanded his followers to remember his death by observing the Memorial annually.
Attending the Memorial is important to show appreciation for the ransom and to proclaim Jesus' death until he comes.
Through faith in this ransom, people can approach Jehovah.

God's Kingdom — A Real Government

God's Kingdom is a heavenly government set up by Jehovah with Jesus as King.
It will rule over the earth and replace all human governments.
“The God of heaven will set up a kingdom that will never be destroyed... It will crush and put an end to all these kingdoms, and it alone will stand forever.” — Daniel 2:44
“Let your Kingdom come. Let your will take place, as in heaven, also on earth.” — Matthew 6:10
God's original purpose was for the earth to be a paradise filled with perfect humans living forever in peace and happiness, as in the Garden of Eden before sin entered.
God's Kingdom will restore that paradise, end suffering, wars, and death, and fulfill Jehovah's loving purpose for mankind and the earth.

False Worship — Distinguishing Truth

The Bible warns against false worship that misrepresents Jehovah.
False worship promotes teachings not based on Scripture.
“You must not have any other gods besides me.” — Exodus 20:3
“Jehovah is near to all those calling on him, to all who call on him in truth.” — Psalm 145:18
True worship honors Jehovah alone, uses His name, and obeys accurate Bible truth.
